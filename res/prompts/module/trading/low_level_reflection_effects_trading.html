Lessons learnt from analysis of price movments can be considered in the following ways:
1. Momentum is a term used in financial market analysis to describe the tendency of asset prices to keep moving in their current direction over time. It is often used to predict short-term price movements based on historical trends. The basic premise of momentum is that securities that have performed well in the past are likely to continue performing well, while those that have performed poorly are likely to continue performing poorly.
